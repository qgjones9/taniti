FAQS

Find answers to your most pressing questions about visiting the beautiful island of Taniti.

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar as its primary currency. Many businesses also accept euros and yen. Currency exchange is facilitated by several local banks.

The legal drinking age in Taniti is 18 years. However, this regulation is not always strictly enforced. Visitors should be aware of local customs regarding alcohol consumption.

In urban areas, many younger Tanitians speak fluent English. However, in rural regions, English is less common, especially among older residents. It's advisable to learn a few basic phrases in the local language.

Taniti has one hospital and several clinics available for visitors. The hospital employs many multilingual staff members. Basic medical services are accessible, but it's wise to have travel insurance.

Violent crime is rare on Taniti, making it a safe destination. However, petty crimes such as pickpocketing have been reported as tourism increases. Visitors should remain vigilant and take standard safety precautions.
